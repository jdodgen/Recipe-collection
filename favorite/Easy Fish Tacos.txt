Easy Fish Tacos

Ingredients  
For the fish:
1 pound white fish filets, # JED about 1.3 oz per taco 
2 to 3 teaspoons chili powder, enough to coat the fish a nice blend is nice any sort of "taco seasoning" works.
1 teaspoon salt leave this out if your "taco seasoning" contains salt.

For the "fish taco sauce":
2 parts sour(ed) cream
1 part mayonnaise
1 part tablespoons lime juice
1/2 part thin hot sauce 

Slaw:
1/2 shredded cabbage (about 6 cups)
some of the "fish taco sauce"

Toppings:
4 radishes, thinly sliced
4 spring/green onions, thinly sliced
1/3 cup roughly chopped cilantro

tortillas: 12 "street taco" size (4 or 5 inches in diameter). Adjust for larger


Method
Make the "fish taco sauce":
In a small bowl, combine the sour cream, mayonnaise, lime juice, and hot sauce. 
Stir until combined. refrigerate.

Make the cabbage slaw:
In a medium bowl, combine the shredded cabbage with 3 tablespoons of the "fish taco sauce". 
Stir to coat and add more sauce if you need. A very thin coating preferred.

Season the fish:
Sprinkle the fish on both sides with chili powder and salt. Gently rub in the seasonings with your fingers to make sure the fish is entirely coated.

Warm the tortillas while cooking the fish or before:

Cook the fish:
Warm a nonstick skillet on the stovetop over medium-high heat. When hot, add a couple of 
tablespoons of olive or other oil to thinly coat the bottom.

Add the fish to the pan and cook and flip.

Use a fork or a knife to gently break the fish into chunks. 

Assemble the tacos with a few pieces of fish, some cabbage slaw, 
toppings of your choice, and a drizzle of the "fish taco sauce".
